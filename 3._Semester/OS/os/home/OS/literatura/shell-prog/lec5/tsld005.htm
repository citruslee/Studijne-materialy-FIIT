Example of Filters in Pipelines

    $ who | grep “^stocker”|more


    execute who, sends its output to grep and look for stocker at the beginning of line in that output, send it to more to display a screenful at a time

    $cat mailmsg|mailx -s “`date` messages” stocker

    display mailmsg to stdin, have mail read stdin and compose a mail message with its contents, put the date in the subject of message

    $mailx -s “`date` messages” stocker<

    same as above but uses input redirection

    $ls *.c |wc -l|sed -n ‘/Total$/p’

    list all *.c files, count lines in file, use sed to find the total

    line and display it
