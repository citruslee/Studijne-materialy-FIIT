Multiple Match (*)

  • Remember * means zero or more.

  • In regular expression the * must be attached to a specific character which you want to match against zero or more

  • When an * appears after a character, it always means zero or more of the character immediately preceding it

    • A* (zero or more A) Q* (zero or more Q)
    • .* (zero or more of ANY character)

  • Most common mistake is to think * is independent of the character is follows and that it stands for one or more rather than zero or more
